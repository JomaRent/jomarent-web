JOMA will retain the personal data collected through the Website only for the time necessary to properly address and manage the user's request, respond to their questions, or fulfill the purpose for which the data was collected.

If, following the initial communication, a contractual relationship is established (e.g., signing a machinery rental agreement), the data will be retained for the duration of that relationship and, afterward, for the period required by applicable laws (e.g., tax and accounting regulations, which typically require documentation to be retained for 4 to 6 years). Once the legally mandated retention periods have expired, the personal data will be securely deleted.

User Rights (ARSULIPO Rights)

The user, as the data subject whose personal data may be processed by JOMA, is entitled to exercise a series of data protection rights at any time. In particular, and in accordance with the provisions of the GDPR, the user may request to exercise the ARSULIPO rights, which include the following:

Right of Access: to know what personal data we are processing about you, the origin of such data, and to obtain information about the processing activities carried out with them. This right allows the data subject to confirm whether JOMA is processing their personal data and, if so, to receive a copy of such data along with complete information regarding the purposes, data categories, recipients, retention periods, and the rights they are entitled to.

  • Right to Rectification: to request the correction of inaccurate, erroneous, or incomplete personal data held by JOMA. If any of your personal data has changed or is simply incorrect, you have the right to have it rectified without undue delay.
  • Right to Erasure: to request the deletion of your personal data in certain circumstances, such as when the data is no longer necessary for the purpose it was collected, when the consent on which the processing was based is withdrawn, or when the data subject believes the data is being processed unlawfully. This right is also known as the "right to be forgotten" and allows the data subject to request the deletion of their data when any of the legal grounds apply.
  • Right to Restriction of Processing: to request that JOMA temporarily suspend the processing of your personal data when certain conditions are met. For example, if the accuracy of your data is contested, while such accuracy is verified; or if the data subject has objected to a processing based on legitimate interest, while it is determined whether JOMA’s legitimate grounds override those of the data subject. During this restriction period, JOMA will store the data securely and only process it for retention or for the establishment, exercise, or defense of legal claims.
  • Right to Data Portability: to receive a copy of your personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format, and to transmit it to another data controller by yourself or, if technically feasible, through JOMA. This right applies when processing is based on the data subject's consent or on a contract, and it is carried out by automated means. The data subject may also request, if technically possible, that JOMA directly transfers such data to the new controller they designate.
  • Right to Object: to object at any time, based on your particular situation, to the processing of your personal data when such processing is based on public interest or JOMA’s or another party’s legitimate interest. In such cases, JOMA will stop processing the data for those purposes unless compelling legitimate grounds prevail over the interests, rights, and freedoms of the data subject, or for the establishment, exercise, or defense of legal claims.

In addition to the above, the user has the right to withdraw their consent at any time. This means that, if the processing of their data is based on consent, they may revoke it, and JOMA must cease processing for the relevant purpose. The withdrawal of consent shall not affect the lawfulness of prior processing based on such consent. Furthermore, the user has the right not to be subject to automated individual decisions, including profiling, that produce legal effects or similarly significantly affect them. However, JOMA confirms that it does not carry out any data processing involving automated decision-making without human intervention or profiling that could have legal or significant effects on the user.

  • Postal mail: by sending a written request (including a copy of your ID, NIE, or equivalent document to verify your identity) to the following address: JOMA Alquiler de Maquinaria S.L., Calle Utiel, 22, 46360 Buñol (Valencia, Spain), indicating on the envelope: “Data Protection – ARSULIPO Rights”.

In this case, JOMA may request additional information or documentation to confirm your identity if there is any doubt about the applicant.

We will handle your request without undue delay and, in any case, within a maximum period of one month from receipt. This period may be extended by up to two additional months if necessary (depending on the complexity and number of requests, as allowed by the GDPR). If so, we will inform you of the extension within the first month. Lastly, we inform you that you have the right to file a complaint with the Spanish Data Protection Agency (AEPD) (https://www.aepd.es), or with another relevant supervisory authority competent in your place of residence if you live outside Spain, especially if you are not satisfied with the outcome of the exercise of your rights.
